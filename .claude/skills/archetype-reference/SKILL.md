# Brand Archetypes Reference

## The 12 Archetypes

Archetypes are universal patterns of behavior and motivation. Brands and individuals naturally express archetypal energies. Most brands have a primary archetype (dominant energy) and one or two supporting archetypes.

---

## Ego Types (Leave a Mark on the World)

### The Innocent

**Motto:** "Free to be you and me"
**Core desire:** To be happy
**Goal:** To experience paradise
**Strategy:** Do things right, be optimistic
**Gift:** Faith and optimism
**Shadow:** Denial, repression, naivety that harms self or others

**Voice characteristics:**
- Optimistic, sincere, wholesome
- Simple, clear language
- Focuses on goodness and possibility
- Avoids cynicism and complexity

**Brand examples:** Coca-Cola, Dove, Nintendo

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you believe in the fundamental goodness of people and situations?
- Is your natural instinct to see the bright side?
- Do others sometimes say you're naive or overly trusting?

---

### The Everyman (Regular Guy/Gal)

**Motto:** "All people are created equal"
**Core desire:** Belonging, connection
**Goal:** To fit in, be accepted
**Strategy:** Be down-to-earth, common touch
**Gift:** Realism, empathy, lack of pretense
**Shadow:** Losing self to blend in, mob mentality, victimhood

**Voice characteristics:**
- Relatable, unpretentious, friendly
- Conversational, accessible language
- Uses "we" and "us" frequently
- Avoids elitism or exclusivity

**Brand examples:** IKEA, Target, Budweiser

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you pride yourself on being approachable and relatable?
- Do you distrust elitism or exclusive clubs?
- Is belonging to a community central to your identity?

---

### The Hero

**Motto:** "Where there's a will, there's a way"
**Core desire:** To prove worth through courageous action
**Goal:** To improve the world through mastery and courage
**Strategy:** Be strong, competent, powerful
**Gift:** Competence and courage
**Shadow:** Arrogance, ruthlessness, always needing a battle

**Voice characteristics:**
- Confident, direct, empowering
- Action-oriented language
- Focuses on challenges and victories
- Can be competitive or combative

**Brand examples:** Nike, FedEx, U.S. Army

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Are you driven to prove yourself through achievement?
- Do you naturally take charge in difficult situations?
- Do you sometimes create battles where none exist?

---

### The Caregiver

**Motto:** "Love your neighbor as yourself"
**Core desire:** To protect and care for others
**Goal:** To help others
**Strategy:** Do things for others, nurture
**Gift:** Compassion, generosity
**Shadow:** Martyrdom, enabling, guilt-tripping

**Voice characteristics:**
- Warm, nurturing, supportive
- Focuses on others' needs
- Generous with praise and encouragement
- Can be protective or parental

**Brand examples:** Johnson & Johnson, Campbell's, TOMS

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you naturally put others' needs before your own?
- Do you feel most fulfilled when helping someone?
- Do you sometimes give to the point of exhaustion?

**Adaptation example:** "The Farmer" — focuses on creating conditions for growth rather than direct intervention. Tends the soil rather than pulling the plant.

## Soul Types (Connect with Others)

### The Explorer

**Motto:** "Don't fence me in"
**Core desire:** Freedom to discover
**Goal:** To experience a more authentic, fulfilling life
**Strategy:** Journey, seek out new things, escape boredom
**Gift:** Autonomy, ambition, self-sufficiency
**Shadow:** Aimless wandering, becoming a misfit, inability to commit

**Voice characteristics:**
- Independent, adventurous, pioneering
- Values authenticity over convention
- Language of discovery and possibility
- Resists constraint and conformity

**Brand examples:** Jeep, Patagonia, REI

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you feel constrained by routine and convention?
- Is the journey more important than the destination?
- Do you struggle with commitment or settling down?

---

### The Rebel (Outlaw)

**Motto:** "Rules are made to be broken"
**Core desire:** Revolution, revenge, or reform
**Goal:** To disrupt what isn't working
**Strategy:** Break the rules, shock, destroy
**Gift:** Freedom, radical thinking
**Shadow:** Crossing into crime, self-destruction, alienation

**Voice characteristics:**
- Provocative, disruptive, bold
- Challenges authority and convention
- Edgy, sometimes aggressive
- Values authenticity over approval

**Brand examples:** Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you instinctively question authority and rules?
- Are you energized by shaking up the status quo?
- Do you sometimes push back just for the sake of it?

---

### The Lover

**Motto:** "I only have eyes for you"
**Core desire:** Intimacy, connection, sensual experience
**Goal:** To be in relationship with people, work, experiences they love
**Strategy:** Become attractive, create emotional and physical appeal
**Gift:** Passion, gratitude, appreciation, commitment
**Shadow:** Obsession, jealousy, losing identity in relationships

**Voice characteristics:**
- Passionate, sensual, appreciative
- Emotionally expressive
- Focuses on beauty and connection
- Creates desire and intimacy

**Brand examples:** Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Häagen-Dazs

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Are passion and beauty central to how you experience life?
- Do you form deep, intense connections?
- Do you sometimes lose yourself in relationships or pursuits?

---

### The Creator

**Motto:** "If it can be imagined, it can be created"
**Core desire:** To create something of enduring value
**Goal:** To realize a vision
**Strategy:** Develop artistic control and skill
**Gift:** Creativity and imagination
**Shadow:** Perfectionism, creative blocks, prima donna behavior

**Voice characteristics:**
- Imaginative, expressive, visionary
- Values originality and self-expression
- Aesthetic sensibility in language
- Can be perfectionistic or precious

**Brand examples:** Apple, Lego, Adobe

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you feel compelled to make things that didn't exist before?
- Is self-expression a core need?
- Does perfectionism sometimes paralyze you?

## Self Types (Structure the World)

### The Jester

**Motto:** "If I can't dance, I don't want to be part of your revolution"
**Core desire:** To live in the moment with full enjoyment
**Goal:** To have a great time and lighten up the world
**Strategy:** Play, make jokes, be funny
**Gift:** Joy, humor, perspective
**Shadow:** Frivolity, wasting time, using humor to avoid real issues

**Voice characteristics:**
- Playful, irreverent, fun
- Uses humor and wit
- Doesn't take itself too seriously
- Can be provocative but not mean

**Brand examples:** Old Spice, M&M's, Dollar Shave Club

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Is making people laugh one of your greatest satisfactions?
- Do you use humor to connect and cope?
- Do you sometimes use jokes to deflect from serious topics?

---

### The Sage

**Motto:** "The truth will set you free"
**Core desire:** To understand the world
**Goal:** To use intelligence and analysis to find truth
**Strategy:** Seek information and knowledge, reflect
**Gift:** Wisdom, intelligence, perspective
**Shadow:** Disconnection from life, ivory tower, endless study without action

**Voice characteristics:**
- Thoughtful, analytical, informed
- Values evidence and clarity
- Educational, sometimes professorial
- Can be abstract or detached

**Brand examples:** Google, BBC, The Economist

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Is understanding the world a primary drive?
- Do you value knowledge for its own sake?
- Do you sometimes analyze when you should act?

**Adaptation example:** "The Pattern Naturalist" — observes systems and connections in the wild rather than abstracting into theory. Wisdom from observation, not just study.

---

### The Magician

**Motto:** "It can happen"
**Core desire:** To understand the fundamental laws of the universe
**Goal:** To make dreams come true, transform reality
**Strategy:** Develop a vision and live it
**Gift:** Finding win-win solutions, transforming situations
**Shadow:** Manipulation, disconnection from reality, becoming a charlatan

**Voice characteristics:**
- Visionary, transformative, catalytic
- Language of possibility and change
- Can seem almost mystical
- Makes complex connections seem simple

**Brand examples:** Disney, Dyson, TED

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you see transformative possibilities others miss?
- Are you drawn to the underlying patterns that govern things?
- Do you sometimes oversell what's possible?

---

### The Ruler

**Motto:** "Power isn't everything, it's the only thing"
**Core desire:** Control
**Goal:** To create a prosperous, successful family, organization, or community
**Strategy:** Exercise power, provide leadership
**Gift:** Responsibility, leadership, competence
**Shadow:** Authoritarianism, rigidity, being controlling

**Voice characteristics:**
- Authoritative, commanding, confident
- Language of leadership and excellence
- Sets standards and expectations
- Can be imperious or domineering

**Brand examples:** Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Microsoft

**Self-identification prompts:**
- Do you naturally take control and set direction?
- Is creating order and structure satisfying to you?
- Do you sometimes become controlling or rigid?

## How to Use This Reference

### Identifying Archetypes

1. **Listen for energy, not labels.** Which archetype does the client naturally express? What would others observe?

2. **Look for patterns across contexts.** Does the same energy show up in how they work, communicate, and relate?

3. **Test recognition vs. aspiration.** "Does this feel like you already?" not "Would you like to be this?"

4. **Note what they reject.** Strong negative reactions to an archetype can be as informative as positive ones.

### Primary + Supporting Model

Most brands express:
- **Primary archetype:** Dominant energy, appears 60-70% of the time
- **Supporting archetype:** Secondary energy, adds dimension, appears 20-30% of the time
- **Occasional third:** Situational energy, appears in specific contexts

The combination creates distinctiveness. "Sage + Caregiver" is different from "Sage + Rebel."

### Adapting to Custom Names

Generic archetype names often feel corporate. Encourage clients to rename their archetypes:

- Caregiver → "The Farmer" (tends conditions, not people)
- Sage → "The Pattern Naturalist" (observes systems in the wild)
- Creator → "The Craftsman" (emphasis on craft, not art)
- Magician → "The Bridge Builder" (connects, doesn't mystify)

The custom name should capture their specific expression of the energy.

### Shadow Work

Every archetype has a shadow — an unhealthy or excessive expression. Identify it not to scare the client but to provide self-awareness:

- Caregiver shadow: Martyrdom, enabling, guilt
- Sage shadow: Analysis paralysis, detachment, ivory tower
- Hero shadow: Always needing a battle, arrogance

Include the shadow in the final archetype description as "what to watch for."
