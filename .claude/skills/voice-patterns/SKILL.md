# Voice Patterns

## What Voice Is (and Isn't)

**Voice** is how you sound — the consistent personality that comes through in your writing regardless of topic or format.

**Tone** is how voice flexes for context. Same voice, different tone: celebratory vs. serious, casual vs. formal.

Voice is the constant. Tone is the variable.

---

## Voice Dimensions

### Formality Spectrum

| Formal | Conversational | Casual |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| "We are pleased to announce..." | "We're excited to share..." | "Big news!" |
| Third person, passive voice | First person, active voice | Fragments, slang |
| Complete sentences always | Contractions, some fragments | Whatever works |

Most thought leader brands land in the conversational middle.

### Energy Spectrum

| Reserved | Warm | Enthusiastic |
|----------|------|--------------|
| Measured, careful | Friendly, approachable | Energetic, exclamatory |
| Understated | Encouraging | Celebratory |
| "This may be helpful." | "I think you'll find this useful." | "You're going to love this!" |

### Complexity Spectrum

| Dense | Accessible | Simple |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Long sentences, subordinate clauses | Mixed length, clear structure | Short sentences, basic words |
| Assumes expertise | Explains without condescending | Explains everything |
| Academic/technical register | Professional register | Everyday register |

## Signature Moves

Signature moves are distinctive stylistic choices that become recognizable as "you."

### Types of Signature Moves

**Structural:**
- Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Opening with a question
- Ending with a callback to the opening
- Numbered frameworks (The 3 Cs, The 5 Ps)

**Punctuation:**
- Em dashes for asides
- Parentheticals for internal dialogue
- Ellipses for trailing thoughts
- Colons before lists or reveals

**Rhetorical:**
- Rhetorical questions as transitions
- "Here's the thing:" as setup
- "In other words" reframes
- Direct address ("you")

**Linguistic:**
- Specific pet words or phrases
- Metaphor families (sports, nature, architecture)
- Contractions or lack thereof
- Sentence fragments for punch

### Finding Signature Moves

1. Read 5+ pieces looking for repetition
2. Count frequency of potential moves
3. Distinguish intentional from accidental
4. Test: Is this recognizably them?

**Threshold:** A true signature move appears in 40%+ of content.

### Signature Move Guardrails

Moves lose power when overused:

| Move | Sweet Spot | Overuse Threshold |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Em dashes | 2-4 per piece | 10+ per piece |
| Single-sentence paragraphs | 1-3 per piece | Every other paragraph |
| Rhetorical questions | 1-2 per piece | 5+ per piece |
| Parentheticals | 3-5 per piece | Every paragraph |

## Voice Analysis Metrics

### Quantitative

**Sentence length:**
- Average words per sentence
- Range (shortest to longest)
- Pattern (consistent vs. varied)

Benchmark: 15-20 words average is conversational. Under 12 is punchy. Over 25 is dense.

**Paragraph length:**
- Average sentences per paragraph
- Use of single-sentence paragraphs

Benchmark: 2-4 sentences is standard. Frequent one-sentence paragraphs = stylistic choice.

**Punctuation frequency:**
- Em dashes per 1000 words
- Parentheticals per 1000 words
- Question marks (especially rhetorical)

**Contraction rate:**
- Percentage of contractible phrases that are contracted
- High (80%+) = casual. Low (20%-) = formal.

### Qualitative

**Register:** Academic, professional, conversational, casual
**Energy:** Reserved, warm, enthusiastic
**Orientation:** First person, second person, third person
**Structure preference:** Prose vs. lists vs. mixed

## Voice Fingerprinting Process

1. **Gather samples:** Minimum 3 pieces, ideally 5-10. Content they're proud of.

2. **Read for impression:** What's the overall feel? Write 3-5 adjectives.

3. **Measure quantitative:** Sentence length, paragraph length, punctuation patterns.

4. **Identify signature moves:** What repeats? What's distinctive?

5. **Catalog phrases:** Words/phrases that appear multiple times.

6. **Note anti-patterns:** What's absent? What do they avoid?

7. **Synthesize:** One-line voice description + voice tags + ratio (polished/conversational).

## Guardrails

### Types of Guardrails

**Never-Do Rules:**
- Things that would be off-brand
- Patterns that contradict the voice
- Language that feels wrong

Examples:
- Never use emojis
- Never open with "I"
- Never use jargon without defining it

**Word Substitutions:**
- Preferred terms vs. avoided terms
- Brand-specific vocabulary

Examples:
- Say "people," not "users"
- Say "approach," not "methodology"
- Say "simple," not "streamlined"

**Quality Checks:**
- Pre-publish scan questions
- Things to verify before sending

Examples:
- Did I use more than 3 em dashes?
- Would I say this out loud?
- Is there a list that should be prose?

## The Read-Aloud Test

The simplest voice check: Read it out loud.

If it sounds like writing, rewrite it.
If it sounds like something you'd say, it's on voice.

This catches:
- Overly formal constructions
- Sentences that are too long
- Words you wouldn't actually use
- Rhythms that don't flow
