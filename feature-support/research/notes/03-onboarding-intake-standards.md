# Source Note: Brand Agency Onboarding / Discovery Intake Standards

**Source ID:** SRC-009
**Type:** Multi-source synthesis (agency practices, questionnaire frameworks)
**Processed:** 2026-03-26
**Credibility:** Moderate-high — triangulated across multiple agency sources
**Sources consulted:** monday.com (intake framework), ManyRequests (brand questionnaire), spp.co (brand questionnaire), Focus Lab (engagement structure), Bethanyworks (translation process), ColorPark (translation framework), Tilda Education / Iconic Fox (archetype-visual mapping)

## Key Findings

### 1. Standard Intake Clusters

Professional agency onboarding consistently organizes intake into 5-6 clusters:

**Business context layer:**
- Budget and timeline expectations (monday.com: core differentiator between "contact form" and real "intake form")
- Engagement goals / success metrics — what does winning look like?
- Entity stage: startup vs. rebrand vs. repositioning
- Previous branding attempts — what was tried, what failed, what the client liked/disliked
- Brand history — how long in operation, major pivots, legacy constraints

**Competitive landscape layer:**
- Explicit competitive set: 3-4 named competitors with URLs
- Current positioning against competitors
- Competitive gaps / white space the client perceives
- Brands (outside category) the client admires and why

**Existing brand perception layer:**
- How current customers describe the brand today (perceived vs. intended)
- What the client is dissatisfied with about current brand
- "What are we NOT" — boundaries and anti-identities

**Marketing history layer:**
- Channels currently active and their performance
- What marketing has worked; what hasn't
- Platform access and credentials

**Audience pre-seeding:**
- Rough ideal customer description before discovery (baseline to challenge later)
- Where the audience currently finds the client

**Visual preference pre-seeding:**
- Imagery style preferences
- Brands the client admires visually
- Contrasting axis questions (Modern/Classic, Masculine/Feminine, Bold/Subtle)
- This pre-seeds Phase 7 before any conversation happens

### 2. Intake vs. Discovery — Structural Distinction

Larger agencies treat these as two separate instruments:
- **Intake form** (pre-project): business context, scope, access, budget
- **Discovery questionnaire** (at project start): purpose, audience, differentiation

Smaller agencies collapse them. Brand Compass Phase 0 is closer to a minimal intake than a full discovery pre-load.

### 3. Focus Lab's Engagement Scoping

Focus Lab structures engagements in timed sprints (6-week, 9-12 week, 12+) based on client's self-assessed clarity about business and customers. Intake must determine which engagement type is appropriate — implies capturing organizational complexity, stakeholder count, and internal alignment status.

## Relevance to Brand Compass

**Phase 0 currently captures:** brand name, entity type, description, platforms, existing assets, asset pack selection (6 elements)

**Standard agency intake additionally captures (not in Phase 0):**
- Named competitors (3-4 with URLs)
- Brand history / previous branding attempts
- Budget/timeline expectations
- Existing brand perception gap (perceived vs. intended)
- Visual preference pre-seeding (admired brands, contrasting axes)
- Engagement goals / success metrics
- Marketing history (what's worked, what hasn't)

**Finding tag:** GAP — Phase 0 captures minimal business context. Standard agency intake includes competitive set, brand history, perception gap, and visual preferences — all of which improve downstream discovery quality.
