# SRC-023: JTBD Switch Interview Methodology

**Source type:** Research methodology / Practitioner guides
**Credibility:** High (multiple practitioner sources with consistent methodology, rooted in Moesta/Spiek/Christensen theory)
**Date accessed:** 2026-03-26
**Sources:** CleverX JTBD interview guide (cleverx.com), Dscout JTBD switch products guide (dscout.com/people-nerds/switch-products-jobs-to-be-done), Dscout JTBD interviewing primer (dscout.com/people-nerds/the-jobs-to-be-done-interviewing-style), Customer Science Group switch interview playbook (customerscience.com.au), ProductPlan JTBD framework (productplan.com), HBS JTBD guide (online.hbs.edu), Strategyn JTBD original framework (strategyn.com)

## Key Findings

### The Switch Interview Structure

Developed by Bob Moesta and Chris Spiek. Focuses on the **switch moment**: the point when a customer decided to change their approach. That moment contains the job with all its context.

**Timeline stages:**
1. **First thought** — initial trigger that something isn't working
2. **Passively looking** — browsing but not committed to change
3. **Event one** — pushes into more serious search
4. **Actively looking** — investing time and energy
5. **Event two** — pushes into decision-making
6. **Deciding** — weighing alternatives
7. **Purchase** — making the decision
8. **Consuming** — using the product/service

### The Four Forces of Progress

Every switch decision involves four competing forces:
1. **Push of the current situation** — dissatisfaction with status quo (what's broken)
2. **Pull of the new solution** — attraction of the alternative (what's promised)
3. **Anxiety of the new** — uncertainty about switching (risk, learning curve)
4. **Habit of the present** — comfort and inertia of current state

Key insight: Push + Pull must exceed Anxiety + Habit for a switch to occur. Understanding all four forces reveals not just why customers buy, but why they *don't* buy.

### Interview Technique — Documentary Style

Switch interviews reconstruct a "documentary-like scene" — going into detail about what the person felt and thought at each step, not just actions:

Sample questions for trigger moment:
- "When did you first start thinking about your purchase?"
- "What was the thought that triggered you?"
- "What time of day was it? Where were you? Who was around?"
- "What were you wearing? What was the weather like?"

The granularity isn't trivial — it forces specificity and prevents post-hoc rationalization. The environment and emotional context reveal the real trigger.

### Sample Size

10-15 switch interviews per customer segment typically surfaces major jobs and forces. Saturation occurs within 15-20 interviews for a well-defined segment.

### When JTBD Switch Interviews Are Most Useful

Per Dscout: "When a stakeholder starts talking about understanding competitors and why users stay with or switch to specific products, my JTBD radar goes off."

Best for:
- Understanding why customers chose you over alternatives
- Understanding why customers left
- Understanding the trigger moments that start the buying journey

NOT best for:
- Pain points and unmet needs (use generative interviews)
- Understanding processes (use journey mapping)
- Usability (use usability testing)

### Relevance to Brand Compass

Brand Compass Phase 2 asks:
- "What moment triggers them to seek help?" (Q7) — this is the closest to the switch interview's trigger concept
- "When they find you and it works, what do they say?" (Q10) — captures the pull force
- "Why you specifically?" (Q11) — captures differentiated value

What Phase 2 captures from the JTBD lens:
- ✅ Trigger moment (Q7 — but as a single question, not a detailed reconstruction)
- ✅ Pull of the solution (Q10, Q11)
- ❌ Push of the current situation (what was broken before?) — partially in Q1 ("struggling with") but not systematically
- ❌ Anxiety of the new (what almost stopped them from hiring you?)
- ❌ Habit of the present (what were they doing before that kept them from switching sooner?)

The four forces model suggests two specific additions to Phase 2:
1. **"What were they doing before you — and why did that stop working?"** (Push + Habit)
2. **"What almost stopped them from choosing you? What made them hesitate?"** (Anxiety)

These would round out the Market of One section from a narrative portrait to a full decision-journey portrait.

### Transferable Element: The "Hiring Moment"

The most transferable JTBD concept isn't the full switch interview (too product-focused for brand work) — it's the framing that customers **hire** brands to do jobs. This reframes Phase 2 from "who is your audience" to "what job does your audience hire you to do?" which is a more actionable strategic input for Phase 3 positioning.
