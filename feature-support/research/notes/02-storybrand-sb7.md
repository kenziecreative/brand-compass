## Framework Summary

The StoryBrand SB7 Framework is a seven-part narrative structure for brand messaging, created by Donald Miller. It adapts Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey to marketing. The central principle: **the customer is the hero; the brand is the guide.**

## The Seven Components

1. **A Character (the customer)** — Identify who the customer is and what they want. The customer is the hero, not the brand.
2. **Has a Problem** — Three levels of problem:
   - **External problem:** The tangible, obvious challenge (e.g., low website traffic)
   - **Internal problem:** The frustration it causes (e.g., feeling stuck)
   - **Philosophical problem:** The bigger "why" (e.g., every business deserves to grow)
   - Key insight: "Customers buy solutions to internal problems" — the emotional dimension drives purchase
3. **And Meets a Guide (the brand)** — The guide demonstrates two qualities:
   - **Empathy:** Understanding the customer's struggle
   - **Authority:** Credibility to solve the problem
4. **Who Gives Them a Plan** — Simple, clear steps (ideally 3) that reduce confusion and risk
5. **And Calls Them to Action** — Two types:
   - **Direct CTA:** "Buy now," "Schedule a call"
   - **Transitional CTA:** Lower-commitment offer (free PDF, webinar)
6. **That Ends in Success** — Paint a clear picture of life after the solution
7. **And Helps Them Avoid Failure** — What happens if they DON'T act (stakes)

## Key Deliverable: The BrandScript

SB7 produces a one-page "BrandScript" — a structured summary of all seven story elements. This is used as the basis for all subsequent messaging: website copy, emails, sales scripts, etc. It's a **messaging filter**, not a full brand strategy.

## What SB7 Covers Well

- Customer-centric messaging (brand as guide, not hero)
- Three-level problem definition (external, internal, philosophical)
- Clear narrative arc with stakes
- Actionable — directly translates to website copy, email, campaigns
- The BrandScript as a one-page synthesis artifact

## What SB7 Does NOT Cover

- Visual identity (no color, typography, mark guidance)
- Brand personality or archetypes
- Voice and tone beyond the narrative framework
- Competitive positioning or differentiation strategy
- Audience research methodology
- Brand purpose/origin/values
- Design systems or deliverable specs

## Explicit Limitation (from Umbrex)

"SB7 is not a substitute for strategy, proof, or media planning — pair it with Message House, IMC, and PESO for orchestration and measurement."

## Relevance to Brand Compass Benchmarking

### Where SB7 Challenges Brand Compass

1. **Customer-as-hero framing:** SB7's central move is positioning the customer as the protagonist. Brand Compass Phase 5 (Messaging) asks for "core narrative" and "brand story" but the framing in the discovery questions is largely brand-outward: "What's the origin story?", "What tension set you on this path?", "What transformation do you make possible?" These are brand-centric questions. SB7 would ask: "What is your customer's story? What problem are they living with? What does their life look like before and after?"

2. **Three-level problem articulation:** SB7's external/internal/philosophical problem framework is explicit and structured. Brand Compass Phase 1 asks "What happens when your belief is ignored? What's the cost?" and "Who suffers most?" — these touch on consequences but don't systematically separate the three problem levels from the customer's perspective. Phase 2 asks about struggles but doesn't use the three-level framework.

3. **Stakes/failure:** SB7 explicitly asks "what happens if the customer doesn't act?" Brand Compass doesn't have a structured "failure state" question in its messaging phase.

4. **One-page synthesis:** The BrandScript is a single-page reference artifact. Brand Compass produces multiple documents but doesn't have an explicit one-page brand canvas/summary.

### Where Brand Compass Is Stronger

1. Brand Compass covers vastly more ground: origin, values, audience segmentation, positioning, personality, archetypes, voice, visual identity, toolkit
2. Brand Compass has a structured discovery process across 8 phases; SB7 is primarily a messaging exercise
3. Brand Compass captures brand personality, voice, and expression; SB7 has no voice/tone system
4. Brand Compass has visual identity output; SB7 is text-only

### Finding Tags

- **COMPLICATED:** Brand Compass's Phase 5 messaging may be too brand-centric; SB7's customer-as-hero framing reveals a potential gap in how the core narrative is structured
- **GAP:** Brand Compass lacks an explicit three-level problem framework (external/internal/philosophical) from the customer's perspective
- **GAP:** No one-page brand canvas/BrandScript equivalent as a synthesis artifact
- **GAP:** No explicit "failure state" / "what happens if they don't act" in messaging discovery
