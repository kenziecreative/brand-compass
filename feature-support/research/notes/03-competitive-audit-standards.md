# Source Note: Competitive Brand Audit Standards

**Source ID:** SRC-008
**Type:** Multi-source synthesis (agency practices, strategy frameworks)
**Processed:** 2026-03-26
**Credibility:** Moderate-high — triangulated across multiple agency sources and strategy frameworks
**Sources consulted:** Lippincott (agency process), Connective Web Design (positioning framework), Spellbrand (positioning framework), Shine Creative (agency process), Sanders & Jay (agency discovery), Fratzke Media (brand audit framework), ArtVersion (visual brand audit), Wolff Olins (strategy approach), SurveyMonkey (competitive positioning guide), Tracksuit (methodology)

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Standard Competitive Audit Components

Three-tier structure appears consistently across agencies:

**Tier 1 — Competitor identification and scoping:**
- Direct, indirect, and aspirational/future competitors
- Standard scope: 3-10 competitors (Tracksuit specifies 3-4 challengers + 1-2 incumbents)
- Treated as a prerequisite — audits without scoping produce confirmation-bias outputs

**Tier 2 — Per-competitor brand analysis:**
- Positioning statement reconstruction (from public evidence)
- Messaging and language patterns
- Visual identity territory (color, typography, mark style)
- Target audience definition
- Value proposition and differentiators (stated vs. implied)
- Marketing/channel strategy
- Pricing architecture signals
- Customer perception signals (reviews, sentiment, trust indicators)

**Tier 3 — Synthesis outputs:**
- Competitive positioning map / perceptual map (2-axis grid)
- White space analysis
- SWOT per key competitor
- Threat assessment (growing/stagnant/entering)

### 2. Required vs. Optional Status

**Finding: Competitive analysis is treated as required, not optional, for rigorous positioning work across all major agency sources examined.**

- Lippincott: "conduct thorough quantitative and qualitative research — including internal audits, competitive analyses and stakeholder interviews" — standard process, no suggestion it's optional
- Connective Web Design: classifies competitive landscape analysis as one of four "essential" components for positioning (not conditional)
- Spellbrand: sequences as Step 1 (market analysis) → Step 2 (competitive landscape) → Step 3 (value proposition) → Step 4 (positioning statement) — competitive comes before positioning
- Shine Creative: Step 3 is "Map the competitive landscape" before Step 4 "define position" and Step 5 "messaging"
- Sanders & Jay: competitive context is standard discovery content

**One nuance:** Connective Web Design notes that in narrow niches where the client "knows all competitors personally," simpler approaches work. Exception, not the rule.

### 3. Standard Depth

**Moderate depth is standard for brand strategy work:**
- Positioning map with 5-10 competitors
- Per-competitor messaging, visual identity territory, audience, value prop
- White space identification
- Strategic implications synthesis

**Deep teardown reserved for:** enterprise rebrandings, M&A, crowded market entry, high competitive stakes. Timeline: 6-12 weeks (Fratzke Media).

**Surface-level (list + feature comparison):** considered inadequate for brand strategy. Common only in DIY/SEO marketing audits.

### 4. Agency-Specific Practices

| Agency | Practice |
|--------|----------|
| Lippincott | Competitive analyses as standard quantitative/qualitative research |
| Wolff Olins | "GamePlan" workshops with existential category questions; competitive context implicit in market disruption focus |
| ArtVersion | Dedicated "Visual Brand Audit" service; Market Research and Brand Analysis as listed disciplines |
| Canny Creative | Brand audits before rebranding; includes brand standing relative to competitors |
| Sanders & Jay | Discovery includes full brand asset audit, customer feedback, and deep market research |
| Fratzke Media | "Competitive and Market Context" section in brand audit: competitive brand analysis, market trends, white space |

## Relevance to Brand Compass

**Direct comparison:** Brand Compass's Research Analyst agent is optional, triggered passively ("client mentions competitors/industry OR you need market context"). Industry standard is that competitive analysis is a required, default step in discovery/positioning work — not conditional on client mention.

**The gap is in the trigger, not the depth.** The Research Analyst's competitive brief output aligns with moderate depth (positioning territories, pricing dynamics, whitespace analysis per the Baseline example). The issue is that it might never run if the client doesn't mention competitors.

**Finding tag:** GAP — Competitive analysis should be structurally required before Phase 3 positioning, not passively triggered.
