## Framework Summary

The Golden Circle is a communication model from Simon Sinek's *Start With Why* (2009) and his TED talk "How Great Leaders Inspire Action" (60M+ views). Core premise: **great organizations communicate from the inside out — starting with WHY, then HOW, then WHAT.** Most organizations do the opposite (What → How → Why), which fails to inspire.

The Golden Circle maps to the brain: WHY communicates with the limbic system (feelings, trust, loyalty, decision-making); WHAT communicates with the neocortex (rational thought, language).

## The Three Rings

### WHY (Center)
Your purpose, cause, or belief. WHY does your company exist? WHY do you get out of bed every morning? **WHY should anyone care?**

"Your WHY is your purpose, cause, or belief. WHY does your company exist? WHY do you get out of bed every morning? And WHY should anyone care about the work you do?"

Not about making money — that's a result. The WHY is what inspires action.

Example: Apple's WHY = "We think differently, thereby challenging the status quo."

### HOW (Middle)
The actions you take when at your natural best, living out your WHY. Your HOWs make you unique. "The combination of your WHY and HOWs is like your organization's fingerprint."

HOWs are often used to explain competitive advantage, but they're more than that — they're the authentic expression of the WHY.

Example: Apple's HOW = "We simplify, we perfect, and we start over until every product meets our standard."

### WHAT (Outer)
The tangible products, services, or results. Everyone knows their WHAT.

Example: Apple's WHAT = Computers, phones, tablets, etc.

## Key Principles

1. **People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it.** The WHY creates emotional connection that drives loyalty.
2. **Communicate from the inside out.** Start with purpose, then method, then offering.
3. **Clarity of purpose is a competitive advantage.** Brands that articulate their WHY attract customers who share their values.
4. **WHY is distinct from mission and vision.** Purpose = why you exist. Mission = how you create the future. Vision = what the future looks like if you succeed.

## Brand Purpose vs. Mission vs. Vision vs. Values

From Fresh Sparks:
- **Purpose:** Why you exist beyond making money
- **Mission:** How you create that future you envision
- **Vision:** What the ideal future looks like
- **Values:** The principles that guide your behavior

## Relevance to Brand Compass Benchmarking

### Where the Golden Circle Challenges Brand Compass

1. **WHY as a distinct output:** Brand Compass Phase 1 captures "core belief" (what the client fundamentally believes) and origin story (where the belief came from). The core belief is closely aligned with Sinek's WHY, but Brand Compass doesn't produce a distinct "WHY statement" as a separate deliverable. The core belief could function as the WHY, but it's not framed that way — it's framed as the brand's conviction, not as the answer to "why should anyone care?"

2. **Inside-out communication structure:** Sinek argues all communications should follow WHY → HOW → WHAT order. Brand Compass's messaging architecture (Phase 5) produces taglines, narratives, and boilerplates, but doesn't explicitly structure them in inside-out order. The boilerplates (one sentence, one paragraph, three paragraphs) grow outward from the offering, not from the WHY.

3. **Purpose vs. Belief distinction:** Brand Compass calls it "core belief"; Sinek calls it "purpose/cause/belief." These overlap but aren't identical. A belief ("I believe people should own their creative process") is a conviction. A purpose ("to empower creative independence") is a directive. Brand Compass captures the belief but doesn't always convert it into a purpose statement. For org entities, it captures mission and vision, but even there the purpose is assumed to be implicit in the belief, not explicitly derived.

4. **HOW as organizational fingerprint:** Sinek's HOW describes "the actions we take when at our natural best." Brand Compass captures values (Phase 1) and personality (Phase 4), which are related but not identical. The HOW is about distinctive method/process; values are about principles; personality is about character. None of these explicitly asks "what is your distinctive way of working that makes you different?"

### Where Brand Compass Aligns Well

1. Phase 1 ("What do you believe about people, work, or your field that others might not?") is a WHY question
2. Phase 1's core belief output is functionally equivalent to the WHY for most practitioners
3. Phase 3's positioning asks about differentiation (related to HOW)
4. The overall sequence (belief → audience → positioning → personality → messaging) follows an inside-out logic at the structural level

### Where Brand Compass Is Stronger

1. Brand Compass is vastly more comprehensive — the Golden Circle is three concentric circles; Brand Compass is 122 discovery questions across 8 phases
2. Brand Compass captures personality, voice, visual identity — none of which the Golden Circle addresses
3. Brand Compass has a structured facilitation process; the Golden Circle is a conceptual framework, not a delivery methodology
4. Brand Compass distinguishes between belief, mission, and vision for org entities

### Finding Tags

- **SUPPORTED:** Brand Compass Phase 1's core belief aligns well with Sinek's WHY
- **COMPLICATED:** Core belief and purpose/WHY overlap but aren't identical — Brand Compass could benefit from explicitly deriving a purpose statement from the core belief
- **GAP:** No explicit "HOW" articulation — the distinctive method/process that makes the brand unique in execution, not just in belief
- **COMPLICATED:** Messaging (Phase 5) isn't explicitly structured in inside-out (WHY → HOW → WHAT) order
