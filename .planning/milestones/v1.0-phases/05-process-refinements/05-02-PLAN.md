<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 1: Anti-anchoring audit of Phases 1-4 discovery questions</name>
  <files>CLAUDE.md</files>
  <action>
Audit every discovery question in Phases 1-4 of CLAUDE.md against three anti-anchoring principles. Apply light-touch rewrites only where the framing genuinely anchors the response — leave well-framed questions alone.

**Principle 1: Open-ended before structured.** If a question jumps straight to a structured format ("Complete this: ___"), check whether the preceding questions have already explored the topic open-endedly. If yes, the structured prompt is fine as a capstone. If not, the structured prompt is doing discovery work it shouldn't — consider adding an open-ended lead-in or reframing.

**Principle 2: Remove leading number specifications.** Replace specific counts with natural language that lets the client determine their own volume:

Specific rewrites needed:
- Phase 2 Q1: "Think of 3-4 different people you've helped" -> "Think of the different people you've helped — as many as come to mind"
- Phase 4 Q1: "how would guests describe them? What 4-6 adjectives?" -> "how would guests describe them? What words come to mind?"
- Phase 4 Q5: "Which 2-3 feel like home" -> "Which feel like home"
- Phase 5 Q1: "In 3-7 words" -> "In a few words"
- Phase 5 Q11: "4-6 specific things" -> "What specific things?"
- Phase 6 Q3: "3-5 non-negotiables?" -> "What are your non-negotiables?"
- Phase 6 Q5: "List 6-10 words" -> "What words describe your voice?"

Leave questions alone where the number serves a structural purpose (e.g., "one sentence" / "one paragraph" / "three paragraphs" in boilerplate length specifications — these define deliverable formats, not discovery anchors).

**Principle 3: Multiple frames rather than single prompts.** Where a question provides only one entry point to a concept, check whether an alternative frame would help clients who don't connect with the first one. Only add alternative frames where the question is genuinely narrow — don't pad already-rich questions.

Specific candidates for alternative framing:
- Phase 1 Q4 "Complete this: 'Most people think ___, but I believe ___'" — this is fine as-is because Q1-Q3 already explore the belief open-endedly. Q4 is a synthesis capstone.
- Phase 3 Q24 "Complete: 'Most experts say ___. I say ___.'" — same pattern, fine as-is.
- Phase 4 Q1 (dinner party metaphor) — add an alternative frame after: "Or if that metaphor doesn't land: what energy does your brand bring to a room?"
- Phase 6 Q4 "Complete: 'I write like a [noun] who [verb phrase].'" — add: "If that format feels forced, just describe how your writing feels — what would someone say after reading your work?"

Do NOT rewrite questions that are already well-framed. The anti-anchoring audit is surgical — most questions in Phases 1-4 are already open-ended and should remain unchanged. Focus edits on the specific items listed above.
