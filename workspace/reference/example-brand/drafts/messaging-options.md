## 1. Tagline Options

### Option A — **"Compliance that scales with you."** [SELECTED]
Straightforward promise. Addresses the core anxiety of scaling companies: that their compliance posture will crack under growth. The word "scales" does double duty — it speaks the language of tech (scalability) while referencing the traditional scales of accounting. Clean, confident, no jargon.

### Option B — "Your back office, built for velocity."
Leans into speed and momentum. Positions Baseline as infrastructure rather than overhead. "Velocity" is borrowed from engineering culture intentionally — it signals that this firm understands how tech teams think and measure progress. Slightly more aggressive tone.

### Option C — "Enterprise-grade compliance. Startup-grade delivery."
Tension-based construction. Names the exact gap Baseline fills: the quality of large-firm compliance delivered with the speed and flexibility startups actually need. Risks feeling like a comparison rather than a standalone identity, but it's direct and immediately clear.

### Option D — "The financial backbone for companies that ship."
Cultural signal. "Ship" is a verb that matters in tech — it means building, releasing, executing. This tagline says: we exist so you can keep doing that. Positions compliance as enabler, not blocker. Slightly narrower appeal — less resonant outside engineering-led orgs.

### Option E — "Audit-ready from day one."
Urgency and preparedness. Short, punchy, action-oriented. Speaks directly to the founder or first finance hire staring down a SOC 2 timeline with no internal infrastructure. Less about ongoing relationship, more about immediate capability. Could work well as a campaign line even if not the master tagline.

## 2. Narrative Angles

### Angle 1 — "The Gap Year"
*Perspective: The growing company that has outgrown DIY but isn't ready for Big Four.*

There's a year — sometimes two — where everything breaks. You closed your Series A. You're hiring fast. Your biggest prospect just asked for your SOC 2 report and you don't have one. Your bookkeeper is a spreadsheet your co-founder built at 2 AM. You're too big for "we'll figure it out later" and too small for a six-figure retainer with a firm that will forget your name between quarterly reviews. Baseline was built for that gap. We've packaged the compliance infrastructure that enterprises take for granted — SOC 2 readiness, PCI compliance, privacy frameworks, clean books — and delivered it in a way that makes sense for a team of twenty that just moved out of a WeWork. No padded timelines. No associate-of-an-associate running your engagement. Direct access to people who've seen your exact stage a hundred times and know exactly what you need next.

### Angle 2 — "Compliance as Product"
*Perspective: Baseline's internal philosophy and approach to the work.*

Most compliance firms treat their work like a checklist. Baseline treats it like a product. Every engagement starts with scoping — not a sales call with a deck, but an actual technical assessment of where you are, what your stack looks like, and what your customers and investors are going to require in the next twelve months. From there, we build a roadmap. SOC 2 Type I by Q3. PCI-DSS alignment before you integrate Stripe Connect. A privacy policy that actually reflects your data flows, not a template pulled from a legal blog. We version our own processes. We document our methodologies. We run retros after every audit cycle. This matters because compliance isn't a one-time event — it's a system that needs to evolve as your product evolves. When you treat compliance like a product, it ships on time, it gets better with each iteration, and your team actually understands what it does.

### Angle 3 — "The First Finance Hire's Best Friend"
*Perspective: The individual operator who just inherited a mess.*

You took the job because you believed in the company. Now you're looking at fourteen months of unreconciled transactions, a payment processor integration that nobody documented, and a board meeting in six weeks where someone is going to ask about audit readiness. Baseline exists for this moment. We've built a specific onboarding track for first finance hires at scaling startups — a 90-day engagement that gets your books clean, your compliance gaps mapped, and your board deck ready with numbers you can actually defend. We won't replace you. We'll make you look like you've been doing this for ten years. Our Bookkeeping Boot Camps aren't lectures — they're working sessions where we sit in your actual accounting system and build the workflows together. By the end, you own the process. We just made sure it was the right one.
