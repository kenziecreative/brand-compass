Imagery Guidelines

Categories

Data Visualizations

Clean charts, graphs, and dashboards that present compliance and financial data with clarity and confidence.

  • Use brand palette: navy for primary data, teal for highlights, coral sparingly for alerts
  • Minimal grid lines and axis labels — let the data breathe
  • No 3D effects, drop shadows, or decorative gradients on charts
  • Prefer bar, line, and area charts over pie charts

Environmental Photography

Modern office spaces with natural light, organized workspaces, and a professional-but-approachable feel. Think WeWork meets Deloitte.

  • Natural lighting from large windows — warm white balance
  • Light wood surfaces, glass walls, clean desks
  • Shallow depth of field for editorial quality
  • No sterile corporate environments or fluorescent lighting

Contextual Elements

Supporting visuals that ground the brand in the world of accounting and technology: monitors showing dashboards, whiteboards with timelines, structured documents.

  • Screens should show clean, realistic UI — not stock mockups
  • Whiteboards and notes should feel authentic, not staged
  • Avoid showing actual client data or identifiable information
  • Keep backgrounds minimal and uncluttered

People

Real people in natural professional settings — collaborative moments, focused work, and casual conversations that convey competence without stiffness.

  • Diverse, authentic representation — no stock photo staging
  • Smart casual dress code — professional but not suit-and-tie
  • Show collaboration: side-by-side screen reviews, whiteboard discussions
  • Genuine expressions — focus and engagement over posed smiles

Avoid

Generic Stock Photography

Handshakes, skylines, puzzle pieces, or any image that could belong to any company. If it looks like a stock photo, it is.

Overly Dark or Moody Imagery

We are professional, not intimidating. Avoid dark-lit rooms, harsh shadows, or visuals that feel cold and unwelcoming.

Cluttered Desks & Messy Environments

Baseline is about structure and clarity. Environments should feel organized and intentional, never chaotic.

Decorative Data Visualizations

No chartjunk, no 3D pie charts, no gratuitous infographics. If the visual does not communicate real information, remove it.

05

AI Generation Prompt

Use the following prompt as a starting point when generating on-brand imagery with AI tools (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, etc.). Adjust parameters as needed for the specific output format.

Reusable Image Prompt

Clean, modern office environment with natural light and light wood surfaces. Professional but not corporate. Glass walls, organized desk with single monitor showing clean data dashboard. Color palette: navy blue (#1B2B4B), teal (#2A9D8F), warm gray (#F8F7F5). Minimal, structured, no clutter. Photography style: shallow depth of field, warm white balance, natural lighting from large windows.

Tip: Append context-specific modifiers like “focused on the monitor,” “two people reviewing a whiteboard,” or “close-up of hands on keyboard” to tailor the output to specific use cases.

06

Visual Principles

01

Structure Over Decoration

Every visual element earns its place. If it does not inform, orient, or support comprehension, it does not belong. Ornamentation without purpose undermines the trust we are building.

02

Whitespace Is Content

Generous spacing signals confidence and control. It gives content room to breathe and communicates that we are not trying too hard. The space between elements is as intentional as the elements themselves.

03

Data Should Look Like Data

Clean visualizations with clear labels, honest scales, and no chartjunk. When we show numbers, they should be immediately legible and trustworthy. No decorative distortion of information.

04

Professional Does Not Mean Boring

Modern and approachable, never stiff. We use color with intention, typography with personality, and layouts that feel current without chasing trends. Credibility and warmth coexist.
