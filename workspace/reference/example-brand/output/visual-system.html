Font Overview & Rationale

Two typefaces. One tension. DM Serif Display carries the weight of institutional credibility — the kind of authority that belongs on an audit report cover. Inter carries the velocity of the technology companies we serve — clean, modern, built for screens. The pairing is the brand.

The Credibility + Velocity Tension

Baseline exists at the intersection of two worlds that rarely speak the same visual language. Traditional accounting uses serif typefaces to signal heritage and trustworthiness. Technology companies use geometric sans-serifs to signal modernity and speed. Most brands in this space pick one side and accept the tradeoff.

DM Serif Display is the credibility anchor. Its high-contrast strokes and classical proportions evoke the authority of established financial institutions. When a headline reads Compliance that scales with you in DM Serif Display, it feels like a statement from a firm that has done this hundreds of times.

Inter is the velocity engine. Designed for screens by Rasmus Andersson, its tall x-height and open apertures ensure legibility in the dense informational contexts that compliance work demands — reports, dashboards, audit checklists, data tables. It feels native to the technology ecosystem our clients inhabit. It does not call attention to itself. It just works.

Together, they resolve the tension: you can trust us with your audit, and we will not waste your time getting there.

Primary Mark

The Baseline mark is an abstract “B” formed from stacked horizontal bars of increasing width. The stepped layers suggest data stacking, foundation building, and upward growth — a direct visual translation of “compliance as growth infrastructure.” The vertical stem on the left anchors the form, while the right side carries subtle curves that resolve the bars into a recognizable letterform at larger sizes.

At 16px (favicon size), a simplified variant removes the curved bowl details and relies on the stepped-bar structure alone for recognition. The mark should never be rendered below 16px height.

Clear Space Rules

The mark needs room to breathe. Minimum clear space equals the height of the smallest bar in the mark on all sides. For the full lockup, maintain the same minimum from the outer edges of the combined unit. This is the minimum — more space is always better.

1x minimum
1x min

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to 1x the height of the topmost bar element on all sides. For the full lockup (mark + wordmark), maintain 1x the mark height on all sides from the outer edges of the combined unit. Never crowd the mark with other visual elements, text, or competing graphics.

Imagery Style

Baseline's visual world is The Modern Office — not aspirational, not nostalgic, but the actual environment where our clients work and where our value is delivered. Documentary photography, not stock photography. Clean environments, not staged ones. Real people working, not posing for the camera. The emotional tone: competent calm. Things are under control. Systems are in place.

The Visual Territory

On the formality axis, Baseline sits between “hoodie and flip-flops” and “suit and tie” — think: clean pullover, well-maintained desk, headphones on the desk because they were just being used. On the technology axis, between “analog craft” and “sci-fi futurism” — a modern laptop running a terminal window next to a notebook with handwritten notes.

This is the world Baseline inhabits and the world Baseline helps its clients build. Nobody is panicking. Nobody is performing. The compliance report will be ready because the process was built correctly from the start.

Data Visualizations

Clean charts and dashboards rendered in the brand palette. The kind of visualization that earns trust at board meetings.

  • Navy for primary data, teal for highlights, coral for alerts only
  • Minimal grid lines — let the data breathe
  • No 3D effects, no gradients on chart elements
  • Prefer line, bar, and area charts over pie charts

Environmental Photography

Glass walls, light wood surfaces, natural light. Modern offices that feel organized and warm — not sterile, not chaotic.

  • Natural lighting from large windows, warm white balance
  • Light wood surfaces, glass walls, clean desks
  • Shallow depth of field for editorial quality
  • No fluorescent lighting or sterile corporate environments

Contextual Elements

Monitors showing clean dashboards, whiteboards with audit timelines, structured documents. Artifacts of real compliance work.

  • Screens should show realistic, clean UI — not stock mockups
  • Whiteboards and notes should feel authentic, not staged
  • Avoid showing actual client data or identifiable information
  • Keep backgrounds minimal and uncluttered

People

Real people in natural professional settings. Collaborative moments, focused work, casual conversations that convey competence without stiffness.

  • Diverse, authentic representation — no stock photo staging
  • Smart casual dress code — professional but not suit-and-tie
  • Show collaboration: side-by-side screen reviews, whiteboard sessions
  • Genuine expressions — focus and engagement over posed smiles
17

Subject Guidelines

What appears and what never appears. These rules protect the brand from visual cliches that would undermine the credibility and specificity Baseline has earned.

Subjects that Appear

  • Real people working — focused on screens, collaborating at whiteboards, reviewing documents side by side
  • Clean workspaces — single-monitor setups with structured data, organized desks, good lighting
  • Data visualizations — dashboards, compliance scorecards, audit timeline views, progress indicators
  • Modern office environments — glass walls, light wood, plants, natural light
  • Structured documents — reports with clear hierarchy, checklists with completed items, policy frameworks
  • Technology in context — laptops with real interfaces, terminals with clean output, collaboration tools

Subjects that Never Appear

Stock Photo Handshakes

The single most overused image in professional services. Communicates nothing specific and signals zero creative thought. Never use.

Gold & Mahogany Aesthetics

Dark wood, leather chairs, fountain pens. The visual language of traditional accounting. Baseline is modern and accessible — the opposite of a corner office.

Generic “Technology” Imagery

Circuit boards, binary code, glowing blue orbs, abstract network diagrams. Our audience builds technology. They know what it actually looks like.

Clip Art & Cartoon Icons

Undermines the precision and credibility our audience requires. If illustrations are used, they must be geometric, minimal, and built from the brand palette.

Busy Patterns & Textures

The brand's visual strength is whitespace and structure. Patterns compete with content and reduce the clarity core to the brand promise.

Dark & Moody Environments

We are professional, not intimidating. Avoid dark rooms, harsh shadows, or visuals that feel cold and unwelcoming. Natural light is non-negotiable.

AI Generation Prompt

A ready-to-use prompt for generating on-brand imagery with Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, or similar tools. Copy and adapt for specific use cases.

Reusable Base Prompt

Clean, modern office environment with natural light streaming through large windows. Light wood surfaces, glass partition walls. Professional but not corporate. Organized desk with a single monitor displaying a clean data dashboard using navy blue (#1B2B4B) and teal (#2A9D8F) accent colors. Warm gray (#F8F7F5) wall surfaces. Minimal clutter, intentional spacing, structured environment. Photography style: documentary feel, shallow depth of field, warm white balance, natural lighting. No stock photo staging, no posed subjects. The feeling of competent calm — things are under control.

Adapt with context modifiers: “focused on the monitor showing a compliance dashboard,” “two people reviewing an audit timeline on a whiteboard,” “close-up of hands typing at a clean desk with a notebook nearby,” “small team meeting in a glass-walled room, 3-4 people, smart casual attire.”

Data Visualization Prompt

Clean, minimal dashboard interface showing compliance metrics. Navy blue (#1B2B4B) background panels with white text. Teal (#2A9D8F) accent for progress bars and positive indicators. Coral (#E8725C) for alert states only. Inter typeface for all text elements. Bar charts and line graphs with minimal gridlines. No 3D effects, no decorative gradients. Professional data visualization style. Warm gray (#F8F7F5) card surfaces. Sharp corners (4px radius). The aesthetic of a well-built analytics platform, not an infographic.

For hero images and marketing, add: “slight perspective angle, ambient glow from the screen, dark environment around the dashboard to emphasize the interface.”
