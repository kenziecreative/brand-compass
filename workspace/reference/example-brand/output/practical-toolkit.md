## Decision Filter

When evaluating opportunities, ask:

1. **Does this serve growing tech companies?** (If the client isn't a technology business navigating compliance, it's not our lane.)
2. **Does this person value infrastructure over shortcuts?** (If they want the cheapest checkbox solution, we're not the right fit.)
3. **Can we deliver with our existing expertise?** (If it requires knowledge outside accounting, compliance, and SaaS finance, we should refer to our partner network.)
4. **Does this build our reputation in the territory we want to own?** (Does it reinforce "compliance as growth infrastructure" or dilute it?)
5. **Do we have the capacity to deliver at our quality bar?** (A rushed engagement that produces subpar work damages our referral engine.)

If it's not a clear yes on 4+, it's a no.

## Asset Pack: Pitch Deck

*Selected during onboarding — this section provides presentation guidelines derived from the brand system.*

### Deck Structure

**Standard investor/client pitch deck — 12 slides:**

1. **Title Slide** — Baseline logo (geometric "B" mark + wordmark), tagline "Compliance that scales with you.", presenter name
2. **The Problem** — The compliance cliff: Big Four too expensive, generalists don't understand tech, software platforms can't replace judgment
3. **The Solution** — Enterprise-grade compliance for startup-stage companies. SOC 2, PCI, financial infrastructure.
4. **How It Works** — Three-step process: Assess → Build → Certify. Timeline graphic showing 8-week path.
5. **Market Opportunity** — Size of the gap between Big Four and generalists. Number of Series A+ startups per year.
6. **Traction** — 200+ SOC 2 audits, 8-week average, 60% referral rate, 98% retention
7. **Case Study** — Jordan's story (Market of One): 35-person SaaS, board asking for SOC 2, completed in 6 weeks
8. **Business Model** — Engagement types, pricing tiers, expansion revenue from ongoing compliance
9. **Competitive Landscape** — Positioning matrix: Big Four (expensive, slow) vs. Generalists (cheap, wrong) vs. Platforms (automated, limited) vs. Baseline (right-sized, expert)
10. **Team** — Founders, key team members, collective Big Four + startup experience
11. **Financial Projections** — Revenue, client growth, unit economics
12. **Ask / Next Steps** — What we're looking for, clear call to action

### Slide Design Guidelines

**Layout principles:**
- Maximum 6 lines of text per slide. If you need more, split the slide.
- One idea per slide. The title should work as a standalone summary.
- Data slides: one chart or metric per slide, with annotation.
- Left-align body text. Center headlines only on title and section dividers.

**Typography on slides:**
- Slide titles: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, 32-40pt, Navy (#1B2B4B)
- Body text: Inter Regular, 18-24pt, Slate (#64748B)
- Data callouts: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, 48-72pt, Teal (#2A9D8F)
- Fine print / sources: Inter Regular, 12pt, Slate at 60% opacity

**Color usage on slides:**
- Background: White (#FFFFFF) or Warm Gray (#F8F7F5) for most slides
- Navy (#1B2B4B) background for section dividers and title slide
- Teal (#2A9D8F) for key metrics, progress indicators, positive data
- Coral (#E8725C) for problem statements, pain points, competitor weaknesses
- Slate (#64748B) for body text and secondary information

**Chart and data visualization:**
- Bar charts: Teal fill, Slate axis labels
- Line charts: Navy primary line, Teal secondary, Coral for benchmarks
- Pie/donut charts: Navy, Teal, Slate segments — never more than 4 segments
- Always include the number on the chart, not just the visual

**Logo placement:**
- Bottom-right corner of every slide, small (approximately 1" wide)
- Full-color on white/light backgrounds, white on navy backgrounds
- Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the "B" mark on all sides

### Presentation Guidelines

**Tone during delivery:**
- Speak like a partner, not a salesperson. "Here's what we've built" not "Here's what we can do for you."
- Lead with the problem, not the solution. Make the audience feel the compliance cliff before presenting Baseline.
- Use specific numbers over adjectives. "8 weeks" not "fast." "200 audits" not "extensive experience."
- If someone asks a question you can't answer, say so. "I'd need to check on that — I'll follow up by tomorrow." Honesty builds more trust than improvisation.

### Business Card

**Standard dimensions:** 3.5" × 2" (US standard)

**Front:**
- White background (#FFFFFF)
- Baseline wordmark top-left, Navy (#1B2B4B)

- Title: Inter Regular, 8pt, Slate (#64748B)
- Email + phone: Inter Regular, 8pt, Slate
- Teal accent line (0.5pt) separating name/title from contact info

**Back:**
- Navy (#1B2B4B) solid background
- Geometric "B" mark centered, White, sized to fill approximately 40% of the card width
- Tagline below: Inter Regular, 7pt, White at 80% opacity: "Compliance that scales with you."

**Paper:** 16pt uncoated stock. No gloss — Baseline is precise and pragmatic, not flashy.

### Letterhead

**US Letter (8.5" × 11"):**

**Header area (top 1.5"):**
- Baseline wordmark top-left, Navy, approximately 1.5" wide
- Company details top-right, right-aligned: address, phone, email, website
- Font: Inter Regular, 8pt, Slate (#64748B)
- Teal accent line (0.5pt) at bottom of header, spanning full width

**Body area:**
- 1" margins left/right, 0.75" bottom
- Default body font: Inter Regular, 10pt, Navy (#1B2B4B)
- Line height: 1.5
- Paragraph spacing: 12pt after

**Footer area (bottom 0.5"):**
- Tagline left-aligned: Inter Italic, 7pt, Slate at 60%: "Compliance that scales with you."
- Page number right-aligned (if multi-page): Inter Regular, 7pt, Slate

### One-Pager / Fact Sheet

**US Letter, single page — designed as a leave-behind after meetings:**

**Layout (top to bottom):**

1. **Header strip** (Navy background, full width, 1.5" tall)
   - Baseline wordmark, White, left-aligned
   - Headline: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, 18pt, White: "Enterprise-Grade Compliance for Growing Tech Companies"

2. **Three-column value props** (equal width, Teal icons)
   - Column 1: SOC 2 Audits — "Average 8 weeks to certification"
   - Column 2: PCI Compliance — "Full PCI DSS assessment and remediation"
   - Column 3: Financial Infrastructure — "Audit-ready books and investor-grade reporting"

3. **Social proof bar** (Warm Gray background strip)
   - "200+ audits completed · 8-week average · 98% client retention · 60% referral rate"
   - Inter Bold for numbers, Regular for descriptions, 9pt, Navy

4. **How it works** (3-step visual with Teal numbered circles)
   - Step 1: Assess — "We evaluate your current state and build a roadmap"
   - Step 2: Build — "We implement systems and collect evidence"
   - Step 3: Certify — "We guide you through audit to certification"

5. **Client quote** (offset with Teal left border)
   - "Baseline got us SOC 2 certified in six weeks. Our last firm quoted four months and twice the price."
   - Attribution: name, title, company

6. **CTA strip** (bottom, Navy background)
   - "Ready to get started?" + website URL + phone number
   - Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, 14pt, White

### Brochure

**Tri-fold (8.5" × 11" folded to 3.67" × 8.5"):**

**Outer panels (what you see when folded):**
- Front panel: Navy background, Baseline mark centered, tagline below
- Back panel: White background, contact details, QR code to website, social links
- Flap panel: "What our clients say" — 2-3 short testimonials

**Inner panels (what you see when opened):**
- Left panel: "The Problem" — the compliance cliff, who we serve (3 segments as brief descriptions)
- Center panel: "The Solution" — services overview with Teal icons and brief descriptions. SOC 2, PCI, Financial Infrastructure, Bookkeeping Boot Camp, Partner Network.
- Right panel: "The Proof" — key metrics (200+ audits, 8 weeks, 98% retention), brief case study, clear next step

**Design principles for all print:**
- Consistent 0.5" margins on all edges (increase to 0.625" for bleed printing)
- Navy for primary headings, Teal for accents and icons, Slate for body text
- Coral used sparingly — only for callouts or "problem" content (what's broken, what to avoid)
- Photos: if used, should feel like "The Modern Office" — clean, organized, glass-and-light-wood. Never stock photos of people in suits shaking hands.
- White space is intentional. Baseline is precise, not cluttered.
