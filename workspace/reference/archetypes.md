# Brand Archetypes Reference Guide

*A framework for discovering and adapting archetypal energy into personal brand identity*

---

## What Are Archetypes?

Archetypes are universal patterns of behavior and motivation that resonate across cultures and time. Carl Jung identified them as "primitive mental images inherited from the earliest human ancestors, present in the collective unconscious."

For brand building, archetypes serve as:

- **A vocabulary** for describing how you show up in the world
- **A resonance check** to identify what feels authentically you
- **A consistency anchor** to keep your brand coherent across touchpoints
- **A differentiation tool** when combined or adapted uniquely

Archetypes aren't boxes. They're energies. Most people have a primary archetype with 1-2 supporting energies. The goal isn't to pick one and perform it—it's to recognize which patterns already describe how you naturally operate, then name them in your own language.

## The 12 Classic Archetypes

Organized by their core orientation: what they're fundamentally seeking.

---

### Ego Types
*Seeking: Safety, structure, and belonging*

---

#### 1. The Innocent

**Motto:** "Free to be you and me"

**Core Desire:** To find paradise, experience happiness

**Goal:** Safety, optimism, returning to a simpler state

**Strategy:** Do things right, stay positive, trust the process

**Gift:** Faith, optimism, simplicity

**Shadow:** Naivety, denial, inability to face complexity

**Fear:** Doing something wrong, being punished, corruption

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Warm, hopeful, uncomplicated
- Sees the good, trusts intentions
- Avoids cynicism and dark complexity

**Brand Examples:** Coca-Cola, Disney, Dove

**You might be an Innocent if:**
- You genuinely believe in people's potential
- You instinctively look for the positive interpretation
- Complexity feels like noise obscuring simple truths
- You want to create safe, welcoming spaces

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Optimist
- The Purist
- The Believer
- The Traditionalist

---

#### 2. The Everyman (Regular Guy/Gal)

**Motto:** "All men and women are created equal"

**Core Desire:** Connection, belonging, fitting in

**Goal:** To be accepted, to belong, to find common ground

**Strategy:** Develop solid virtues, be relatable, stay grounded

**Gift:** Realism, empathy, authenticity, lack of pretense

**Shadow:** Losing self to blend in, superficial relationships, fear of standing out

**Fear:** Being left out, standing out, pretension

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Conversational, unpretentious, accessible
- "We're in this together" energy
- Avoids jargon, elitism, showing off

**Brand Examples:** IKEA, Target, Budweiser

**You might be an Everyman if:**
- You value being approachable over being impressive
- You instinctively translate complex ideas into plain language
- Pretension and exclusivity make you uncomfortable
- You believe wisdom exists everywhere, not just in experts

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Neighbor
- The Citizen
- The Realist
- The Common-Sense Voice

---

#### 3. The Hero

**Motto:** "Where there's a will, there's a way"

**Core Desire:** To prove worth through courageous action

**Goal:** Mastery, achievement, improving the world through effort

**Strategy:** Be strong, competent, capable; rise to challenges

**Gift:** Courage, competence, discipline, determination

**Shadow:** Arrogance, ruthlessness, always needing a battle

**Fear:** Weakness, vulnerability, being ineffective, quitting

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Confident, action-oriented, direct
- "We can do this" energy
- Challenges the audience to rise

**Brand Examples:** Nike, FedEx, U.S. Army

**You might be a Hero if:**
- You instinctively frame things as challenges to overcome
- You believe effort and will can solve most problems
- You're energized by obstacles, not defeated by them
- You want to inspire people to act, not just think

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Warrior
- The Champion
- The Competitor
- The Rescuer

---

#### 4. The Caregiver

**Motto:** "Love your neighbor as yourself"

**Core Desire:** To protect and care for others

**Goal:** To help, serve, nurture those in need

**Strategy:** Do things for others, anticipate needs, sacrifice

**Gift:** Compassion, generosity, nurturing

**Shadow:** Martyrdom, enabling, being exploited, guilt-tripping

**Fear:** Selfishness, ingratitude, being seen as uncaring

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Warm, supportive, reassuring
- "Let me help you" energy
- Focuses on the other person's needs and wellbeing

**Brand Examples:** Johnson & Johnson, Campbell's, TOMS

**You might be a Caregiver if:**
- You instinctively notice what others need
- Your satisfaction comes from others' success
- You naturally create supportive environments
- You measure your impact by who you've helped

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Nurturer
- The Guardian
- The Helper
- The Supporter

---

### Soul Types
*Seeking: Freedom, authenticity, and meaning*

---

#### 5. The Explorer

**Motto:** "Don't fence me in"

**Core Desire:** Freedom to discover, find yourself through experience

**Goal:** A more authentic, fulfilling life through exploration

**Strategy:** Journey, seek new experiences, escape conformity

**Gift:** Autonomy, ambition, independence, curiosity

**Shadow:** Aimless wandering, inability to commit, chronic dissatisfaction

**Fear:** Being trapped, conformity, inner emptiness, missing out

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Adventurous, questioning, independent
- "What's out there?" energy
- Resists convention, seeks frontiers

**Brand Examples:** Jeep, Patagonia, REI, National Geographic

**You might be an Explorer if:**
- You're drawn to edges and frontiers, not centers
- Routine feels like death; novelty feels like oxygen
- You learn by doing, not by studying
- You want to open doors, not close them

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Seeker
- The Pioneer
- The Wanderer
- The Adventurer

---

#### 6. The Rebel (Outlaw)

**Motto:** "Rules are made to be broken"

**Core Desire:** Revolution, liberation, radical change

**Goal:** Overturn what isn't working, destroy the broken system

**Strategy:** Disrupt, shock, break rules, challenge authority

**Gift:** Radical freedom, courage to break norms, catalyzing change

**Shadow:** Destruction for its own sake, self-destruction, crime, nihilism

**Fear:** Being powerless, co-opted, ineffective, becoming the establishment

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Provocative, irreverent, challenging
- "This is broken, let's tear it down" energy
- Questions assumptions, attacks sacred cows

**Brand Examples:** Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel

**You might be a Rebel if:**
- You instinctively question authority and convention
- "That's how it's done" makes you want to do the opposite
- You're energized by being underestimated
- You see liberation in breaking comfortable patterns

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Disruptor
- The Revolutionary
- The Contrarian
- The Iconoclast

---

#### 7. The Lover

**Motto:** "You're the only one"

**Core Desire:** Intimacy, connection, sensory experience

**Goal:** Deep relationships with people, work, and environment

**Strategy:** Become attractive (physically, emotionally), create closeness

**Gift:** Passion, appreciation, commitment, aesthetic sense

**Shadow:** Losing identity to please others, obsession, jealousy

**Fear:** Being alone, unwanted, unloved, unworthy of love

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Warm, intimate, sensory, appreciative
- "Experience this deeply" energy
- Evokes feeling, beauty, connection

**Brand Examples:** Chanel, Godiva, Victoria's Secret, Häagen-Dazs

**You might be a Lover if:**
- You're drawn to beauty, texture, and sensory richness
- You crave deep connection over broad networks
- You want people to *feel* something, not just understand
- Commitment and devotion come naturally

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Sensualist
- The Aesthete
- The Romantic
- The Connoisseur

---

#### 8. The Creator

**Motto:** "If you can imagine it, it can be done"

**Core Desire:** To create something of enduring value

**Goal:** Realize a vision, give form to ideas, make the new

**Strategy:** Develop skill and control, experiment, iterate

**Gift:** Creativity, imagination, vision, craftsmanship

**Shadow:** Perfectionism, creative block, impractical visions

**Fear:** Mediocrity, failed execution, inauthenticity, being derivative

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Visionary, inventive, aesthetically attuned
- "Let's make something" energy
- Values originality and craft

**Brand Examples:** Apple, Lego, Adobe, Crayola

**You might be a Creator if:**
- You're compelled to make things—ideas into reality
- You have strong opinions about how things should be crafted
- Derivative work bothers you; originality matters
- You're energized by the act of creation itself

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Maker
- The Artisan
- The Inventor
- The Builder

---

### Self Types
*Seeking: Understanding, transformation, and mastery*

---

#### 9. The Jester

**Motto:** "You only live once"

**Core Desire:** To live fully, enjoy the moment, bring joy

**Goal:** Have a great time, lighten the world, cut through pretense

**Strategy:** Play, make jokes, be present, don't take things too seriously

**Gift:** Joy, humor, perspective, lightness

**Shadow:** Frivolity, irresponsibility, cruelty disguised as humor

**Fear:** Being bored, boring others, being trapped in seriousness

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Playful, irreverent, surprising
- "Lighten up" energy
- Uses humor to reveal truth

**Brand Examples:** M&Ms, Old Spice, Dollar Shave Club

**You might be a Jester if:**
- You instinctively use humor to connect and disarm
- Pretension and self-importance invite your mockery
- You believe play is serious business
- You see absurdity clearly and can't help naming it

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Comedian
- The Provocateur
- The Entertainer
- The Trickster

---

#### 10. The Sage

**Motto:** "The truth will set you free"

**Core Desire:** To find and share truth, understand the world

**Goal:** Use intelligence and analysis to understand reality

**Strategy:** Seek knowledge, reflect, study, teach

**Gift:** Wisdom, intelligence, perspective, insight

**Shadow:** Paralysis by analysis, ivory tower, cold detachment

**Fear:** Ignorance, being misled, superficiality, being wrong

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Thoughtful, analytical, authoritative but curious
- "Here's what's really going on" energy
- Values truth over comfort

**Brand Examples:** Google, The Economist, Harvard, TED

**You might be a Sage if:**
- You're compelled to understand *why*, not just *what*
- You'd rather be right than popular
- You naturally see patterns others miss
- Teaching feels like a natural extension of learning

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Scholar
- The Teacher
- The Analyst
- The Detective
- The Pattern Naturalist

---

#### 11. The Magician

**Motto:** "I make things happen"

**Core Desire:** Understand fundamental laws, transform reality

**Goal:** Make dreams come true, catalyze change, create transformation

**Strategy:** Develop vision, find leverage points, work with unseen forces

**Gift:** Finding win-win solutions, transformation, vision

**Shadow:** Manipulation, disconnection from reality, unintended consequences

**Fear:** Negative unintended consequences, being manipulative

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Visionary, transformative, slightly mysterious
- "Watch this change everything" energy
- Works with systems and leverage points

**Brand Examples:** Disney (theme parks), Polaroid, Dyson, TED

**You might be a Magician if:**
- You see the levers that transform situations
- You're drawn to moments of breakthrough and change
- You believe the right intervention at the right time changes everything
- You work with systems, not just symptoms

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Catalyst
- The Transformer
- The Alchemist
- The Bridge Builder

---

#### 12. The Ruler

**Motto:** "Power isn't everything, it's the only thing"

**Core Desire:** Control, create order and prosperity

**Goal:** Build a successful organization, family, or community

**Strategy:** Exercise leadership, take responsibility, set standards

**Gift:** Responsibility, leadership, creating prosperity

**Shadow:** Authoritarianism, rigidity, inability to delegate

**Fear:** Chaos, being overthrown, losing control

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Authoritative, confident, commanding
- "Here's how it should be done" energy
- Sets standards, expects excellence

**Brand Examples:** Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Microsoft, American Express

**You might be a Ruler if:**
- You instinctively take charge and set direction
- You believe good leadership creates the conditions for others
- Standards and excellence matter to you
- You're comfortable with power and responsibility

**Adapted Versions:**
- The Leader
- The Executive
- The Standard-Setter
- The Conditions Builder

## Using Archetypes for Brand Building

### Step 1: Identify Resonance (Don't Perform, Recognize)

Read through all 12. Notice which descriptions make you think "that's me" versus "I wish that were me." The goal is recognition, not aspiration.

**Questions to ask:**
- Which 2-3 feel like home?
- Which do I default to under pressure?
- Which would people who know me identify?
- Which shows up in my work naturally?

### Step 2: Name Your Primary + Supporting Energies

Most personal brands have:
- **One primary archetype** (60-70% of energy)
- **One or two supporting archetypes** (30-40% combined)

The combination creates uniqueness. "Sage" is common. "Sage + Rebel" is specific. "Sage + Caregiver" is different still.

### Step 3: Adapt into Your Language

The classic 12 are starting points, not destinations. Rename them to fit your specific expression.

**Examples of adaptation:**

| Classic | Adapted | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Caregiver | The Farmer | Focus on conditions, not direct helping |
| Sage | The Pattern Naturalist | Emphasis on observation and systems |
| Magician | The Bridge Builder | Focus on connection, not transformation |
| Hero | The Sherpa | Guide through challenges, not fight them |
| Creator | The Craftsman | Emphasis on skill and iteration |
| Sage | The Detective | Focus on investigation, not teaching |

### Step 4: Define Behavior, Not Just Label

For each archetype, specify:
- **What you do** (observable behavior)
- **What you enable** (outcome for your audience)
- **What you avoid** (the shadow to guard against)

## Archetype Combinations

Some combinations create powerful brand positions:

| Combination | Energy | Example Expression |
|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| Sage + Rebel | "Truth that challenges" | Contrarian thought leadership |
| Creator + Caregiver | "Making things that serve" | Human-centered design |
| Explorer + Sage | "Discovering and explaining" | Investigative teaching |
| Magician + Everyman | "Transformation made accessible" | Practical change-making |
| Hero + Caregiver | "Fighting for others" | Advocacy, protection |
| Jester + Sage | "Truth through humor" | Comedic insight |
| Ruler + Creator | "Building institutions" | Organizational design |

**Tension combinations** (harder to hold, but distinctive):

| Combination | Tension | Resolution |
|-------------|---------|------------|
| Innocent + Rebel | Optimism vs. disruption | Hopeful revolution |
| Ruler + Jester | Control vs. play | Confident irreverence |
| Lover + Sage | Feeling vs. thinking | Emotional intelligence |
| Explorer + Ruler | Freedom vs. structure | Structured adventure |

## Shadow Work

Every archetype has a shadow—the unhealthy expression of its energy. Good brand work acknowledges the shadow and guards against it.

| Archetype | Shadow Expression | Guard Against |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Innocent | Denial, naivety | Ignoring real complexity |
| Everyman | Losing self, conformity | Being bland to belong |
| Hero | Arrogance, combat addiction | Needing enemies |
| Caregiver | Martyrdom, enabling | Helping that disempowers |
| Explorer | Aimlessness, escapism | Wandering without purpose |
| Rebel | Destruction, nihilism | Breaking without building |
| Lover | Obsession, identity loss | Losing self to please |
| Creator | Perfectionism, impracticality | Never shipping |
| Jester | Frivolity, cruelty | Humor that wounds |
| Sage | Paralysis, coldness | Thinking without doing |
| Magician | Manipulation, hubris | Unintended consequences |
| Ruler | Authoritarianism, rigidity | Control that stifles |

## Document Usage

This reference supports **Section 5b: Archetypes** in the Brand Foundation & Messaging document.

**In discovery, use this document to:**
1. Present the 12 archetypes as a vocabulary
2. Guide identification of 2-3 resonant types
3. Facilitate adaptation into custom archetypes
4. Define behavioral expressions and shadow guards

**Output format for final brand document:**

```
### Archetypes

**Primary: [Custom Name]** (adapted from [Classic])
- Behavior: [What you do]
- Enables: [What this creates for your audience]
- Shadow guard: [What you avoid]

**Supporting: [Custom Name]** (adapted from [Classic])
- Behavior: [What you do]
- Enables: [What this creates for your audience]
- Shadow guard: [What you avoid]
```
